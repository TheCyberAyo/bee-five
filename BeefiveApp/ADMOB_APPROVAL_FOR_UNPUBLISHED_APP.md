# Getting AdMob Approval for an Unpublished (Local) App

## Good News! ✅

**You CAN get AdMob approval even if your app is not yet published!** AdMob approves apps based on:
- ✅ Your account status
- ✅ App configuration (package name, ad units)
- ✅ Privacy policy (must be publicly accessible)
- ✅ Policy compliance

**You do NOT need:**
- ❌ App to be in Google Play Store
- ❌ App to be in Apple App Store
- ❌ Public app download link

## What AdMob Needs

### 1. ✅ Account Approval (You Have This!)
- Your AdMob account is already approved
- This is the first step and you're done with it!

### 2. ✅ App Created in AdMob
- Create app in AdMob console (with correct package name)
- Create ad units
- Configure everything properly

### 3. ✅ Privacy Policy (Required - Needs Public URL)
This is the ONLY thing that needs to be publicly accessible online:

**Requirements:**
- Must be a publicly accessible URL (web page)
- Must include information about:
  - Data collection
  - Third-party services (AdMob/Google)
  - How data is used
  - User rights (GDPR, CCPA)
  - Contact information

**Free Hosting Options:**
- GitHub Pages (free, easy)
- Netlify (free, easy)
- Vercel (free, easy)
- Google Sites (free, easy)
- Your own website

### 4. ✅ App Configuration
- Package name matches your code
- App ID added to your app
- Ad units created
- No policy violations

## The Privacy Policy Requirement

**This is the key point:** AdMob doesn't need your app to be public, but they DO need your privacy policy to be publicly accessible online.

### Why This Is Required

AdMob requires a privacy policy URL because:
- It's required by law (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)
- Users need to know how their data is used
- AdMob needs to verify you have one before serving ads

### What You Need to Do

1. **Create a Privacy Policy** (see template below)
2. **Host it online** (free options below)
3. **Get the public URL**
4. **Add it to AdMob** (Apps → Your App → App settings → Privacy Policy URL)

## How to Create & Host Privacy Policy

### Step 1: Create Privacy Policy Content

You can use a privacy policy generator or write your own. Here's what to include:

**Required Sections:**
1. Introduction - What your app is, who you are
2. Data Collection - What data you collect (if any)
3. Third-Party Services - Mention AdMob, Google Analytics, etc.
4. Data Usage - How you use collected data
5. User Rights - GDPR/CCPA rights
6. Contact Information - How to reach you
7. Children's Privacy - If applicable

### Step 2: Host It Online

**Option A: GitHub Pages (Easiest)**

1. Create a GitHub repository
2. Create an `index.html` file with your privacy policy
3. Enable GitHub Pages in repository settings
4. Get your public URL (e.g., `https://yourusername.github.io/privacy-policy`)

**Option B: Google Sites (Very Easy)**

1. Go to [Google Sites](https://sites.google.com)
2. Create a new site
3. Paste your privacy policy content
4. Publish it
5. Get the public URL

**Option C: Netlify/Vercel**

1. Create an HTML file with your privacy policy
2. Deploy to Netlify or Vercel
3. Get the public URL

### Step 3: Add URL to AdMob

1. Go to AdMob Console
2. Apps → Select your app
3. App settings tab
4. Scroll to "Privacy Policy URL"
5. Paste your privacy policy URL
6. Save

## Privacy Policy Template

Here's a basic template you can use:

```html
<!DOCTYPE html>
<html>
<head>
    <title>Privacy Policy - Bee-Five</title>
</head>
<body>
    <h1>Privacy Policy</h1>
    <p>Last updated: [Date]</p>
    
    <h2>1. Introduction</h2>
    <p>Bee-Five ("we", "our", "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your information when you use our mobile application.</p>
    
    <h2>2. Information We Collect</h2>
    <p>We may collect certain information automatically when you use our app, including:</p>
    <ul>
        <li>Device information</li>
        <li>Usage data</li>
        <li>Advertising identifiers</li>
    </ul>
    
    <h2>3. Third-Party Services</h2>
    <p>Our app uses third-party services that may collect information:</p>
    <ul>
        <li><strong>Google AdMob:</strong> We use Google AdMob to serve advertisements. AdMob may collect and use your advertising ID and other information. For more information, please visit Google's Privacy Policy.</li>
    </ul>
    
    <h2>4. How We Use Your Information</h2>
    <p>We use collected information to:</p>
    <ul>
        <li>Provide and maintain our app</li>
        <li>Display personalized advertisements</li>
        <li>Analyze app usage</li>
    </ul>
    
    <h2>5. Your Rights</h2>
    <p>You have the right to:</p>
    <ul>
        <li>Access your personal data</li>
        <li>Request deletion of your data</li>
        <li>Opt-out of targeted advertising</li>
    </ul>
    
    <h2>6. Contact Us</h2>
    <p>If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, contact us at: [Your Email]</p>
    
    <h2>7. Changes to This Policy</h2>
    <p>We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page.</p>
</body>
</html>
```

## Timeline

- **Account approval:** Already done ✅
- **App approval:** Usually 1-7 days after you submit (once privacy policy is added)
- **Ad serving:** Can start immediately after app approval

## Important Notes

### What AdMob Reviews:

1. **Your account** - Already approved ✅
2. **App configuration** - Package name, ad units
3. **Privacy policy** - Must be publicly accessible
4. **Ad implementation** - No policy violations
5. **Traffic quality** - After you start serving ads

### What AdMob Doesn't Require:

- ❌ App in app stores
- ❌ Public download link
- ❌ Published app
- ❌ App reviews or ratings

### After Approval:

Once your app is approved, you can:
- ✅ Test ads in your local app
- ✅ Use production ad unit IDs
- ✅ Start earning revenue
- ✅ Publish to stores later (everything stays the same)
